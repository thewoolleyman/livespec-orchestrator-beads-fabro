## Proposal: Approval is the pending-approval → ready transition; the admission valve is mechanical

### Target specification files

- SPECIFICATION/contracts.md
- SPECIFICATION/scenarios.md
- SPECIFICATION/spec.md

### Summary

Restore the locked cross-repo approval model: `approve` IS the `pending-approval → ready` transition (manual: a human; auto: automatic at capture/groom time), being in `ready` MEANS approved-to-start, and the admission valve (`ready → active`) is purely MECHANICAL (capacity, dependencies, assignee — never permission). Reverts the v023 valve-side-approval text in contracts.md to the v020 model, re-expresses every statement that carried the drift (Scenarios 10, 23, 31, 33; the Dispatcher grooming clauses; the full-autonomous-mode collapse point in contracts.md and spec.md), ratifies a new `## Work-item state semantics` section defining all seven states with the maintainer's recorded rationale, ratifies the dropped `admission_policy` invariant into the doctor-checkable invariants block, and adds intent-preservation clauses so ratified semantics can never again be silently rewritten toward the shipped implementation. Also adds (maintainer-approved 2026-07-04) the `set-admission:` / `set-acceptance:` policy-edit operator actions to the `orchestrate` human-valve surface, governed by the no-surprise-transitions rule: a policy edit never moves an item between states.

### Motivation

Maintainer-ordered replacement (2026-07-04) for the rejected proposal `pending-approval-to-ready-structural-gate-ownership` (archived with its rejection at `SPECIFICATION/history/v027/proposed_changes/`). The cross-repo design of record lives in repo `thewoolleyman/livespec` at `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/research/03-decision-log.md` (decisions 26 and 32), with the maintainer's verbatim reasoning in `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/conversation/transcript.md`: `approve` is the `pending-approval → ready` transition ("manual: a human; auto: automatic"); approval ≡ `ready` membership; the admission valve is purely mechanical ("Permission was settled upstream."); and "`admission_policy` governs only the `approve` routing (auto vs. human)" — an invariant never ratified into this spec tree. How the drift happened: `SPECIFICATION/history/v020/contracts.md` §"Admission valve" encoded the model FAITHFULLY ("By the time an item is `ready` it is, by definition, already approved … Permission was settled upstream at the `pending-approval → ready` (`approve`) transition"), but the SAME v020 revision ratified Scenario 23 in valve-hold form (a re-expression of the legacy Scenario-10 human-gated refusal), contradicting the contracts section beside it. Slice S3 then implemented the scenario, and the v023 revise resolved the contradiction TOWARD the shipped code, rewriting the faithful contract text; the rejected proposal would have completed that drift. Per the maintainer's ruling, this replacement must not only restore the original model — it must "fix whatever the gaps were to clearly describe the intent and ensure that it is preserved" so "this sort of thing doesn't happen in the future": intent preservation is a first-class requirement of this proposal, equal in weight to the model restoration.

### Proposed Changes

The locked model this proposal ratifies (design record: repo `thewoolleyman/livespec`, `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/research/03-decision-log.md`, decisions 26 and 32): `approve` IS the `pending-approval → ready` transition — manual: a human; auto: automatic. Being in `ready` MEANS approved-to-start (approval ≡ `ready` membership). The admission valve (`ready → active`) is purely MECHANICAL — dependencies clear, a free WIP slot, an assignee resolvable; permission was settled upstream at `approve`. `admission_policy` MUST govern only the `approve` routing (auto vs. human); the Dispatcher MUST NOT hold items at `ready` awaiting a human, and an item awaiting a human's permission MUST NOT be in `ready`.

**A. Restore the original valve semantics** — `SPECIFICATION/contracts.md` §"Dispatcher admission, WIP cap, and post-merge acceptance":

A1. The section intro currently reads "Two human-delegable policy valves bracket the WIP-limited autonomous middle of the lifecycle — **admission** (`ready → active`) and **acceptance** (`acceptance → done`). The **Dispatcher (`dispatcher.py` `dispatch`/`loop`) is the sole enforcer** of both valves and the WIP cap; the console only commands (a human triggers `approve` for a manual item, through the `orchestrate` human-valve actions) and observes — it never enforces." It MUST be replaced with: "Two human-delegable policy gates bracket the WIP-limited autonomous middle of the lifecycle — **`approve`** (`pending-approval → ready`) and **`accept`** (`acceptance → done`). The **Dispatcher (`dispatcher.py` `dispatch`/`loop`) is the sole enforcer** of the mechanical admission valve (`ready → active`), the WIP cap, and the acceptance valve; the console only commands (a human triggers `approve` for a manual item resting at `pending-approval`, through the `orchestrate` human-valve actions) and observes — it never enforces." The closing sentence's "the state machine + the valve semantics are the cross-repo design of record" MUST gain the repo-qualified citation "(repo `thewoolleyman/livespec`, `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/research/03-decision-log.md`, decisions 26/32)".

A2. §"Admission valve (`ready → active`)" MUST be reverted to the v020 model (restoring `SPECIFICATION/history/v020/contracts.md` §"Admission valve" while preserving the later, unrelated rank-ordering refinement). Resulting text: "By the time an item is `ready` it is, by definition, already approved (approval ≡ `ready` membership — entering `ready` IS approving; see §"Work-item state semantics"). So the valve's remaining conditions are purely mechanical — capacity, dependencies, and a resolvable assignee:" followed by three bullets: (permission) "**Permission** was settled upstream at the `pending-approval → ready` (`approve`) transition, governed by the item's effective `admission_policy` (`item.admission_policy`, with `None` inheriting the safe default `manual`): `auto` auto-approves once into `ready` at capture/groom time; `manual` (the default, via inherit) rests at `pending-approval` until a human's explicit `approve`. The `admission_policy` field is the first-class realization that **replaces the prior `host-only` / `human-gated` text markers** — risky / irreversible work is held at the `approve` gate (resting at `pending-approval`), never by a pre-merge acceptance gate. The Dispatcher MUST NOT hold an item at `ready` awaiting a human — an item awaiting a human's permission MUST NOT be in `ready`."; (capacity and assignee bullets unchanged). The closing paragraph MUST read: "The Dispatcher MUST, when a WIP slot frees, admit the **top-ranked** (lexicographically earliest `rank`, per §"Work-item beads-issue mapping") admission-eligible `ready` item (eligible = dependencies clear AND an assignee is resolvable — `admission_policy` plays no part at this valve), set its `assignee` (the reused field, not a new `owner`), and transition it to `active`. The Dispatcher MUST NOT auto-approve (`pending-approval → ready`) an item whose effective `admission_policy` is `manual`; it MUST surface the resting item for the maintainer's explicit `approve` on every pass (independent of capacity)." The section's final exercised-by sentence MUST rename "the manual-admission hold" to "the manual rest-at-`pending-approval`".

A3. §"`orchestrate`" → "Human valve actions": the description "`approve:<work-item-id>` (explicitly admits an effective-`manual` `ready` item: sets `assignee` and transitions it to `active`, subject to the per-repo WIP cap)" MUST be replaced with "`approve:<work-item-id>` (the human approval act: transitions an effective-`manual` item from `pending-approval` to `ready`; admission to `active` then follows mechanically when a WIP slot frees, dependencies are clear, and an assignee resolves)". `accept:` and `reject:` are unchanged. The same amendment MUST add two policy-edit operator action ids to the published surface (maintainer-approved 2026-07-04): `set-admission:<work-item-id>:auto|manual` and `set-acceptance:<work-item-id>:ai-only|human-only|ai-then-human`. Each MUST modify ONLY the named policy field of an existing item (realized on beads as the `admission:` / `acceptance:` label through the store seam) and MUST NOT change the item's status; the operator's explicit action selection is the consent (per §"Store-write consent discipline" → up-front operation decision); the journal MUST record the actor; the console invokes these actions and never writes the ledger directly. Motivation: today no surface can edit an existing item's policies — the only mechanism is a raw `bd update` label write that bypasses every consent surface; these actions close that hole.

A4. The no-surprise-transitions rule, stated normatively in the same "Human valve actions" amendment beside the policy-edit actions: a policy edit NEVER moves an item between states. In particular, flipping an item's `admission_policy` from `manual` to `auto` while it rests at `pending-approval` MUST NOT approve it into `ready` — the automatic GO fires only once, at capture/groom time (the locked design's auto-defer rule); after a later policy flip, moving the item still requires an explicit `approve:<work-item-id>`. Symmetrically, flipping `auto` to `manual` on an item already at `ready` MUST NOT demote it — it was already approved; only an explicit defer takes an item out of `ready`.

**E. Add a ratified state-semantics section** — ONE new `## ` H2 in `SPECIFICATION/contracts.md`, "## Work-item state semantics", inserted immediately BEFORE §"Dispatcher admission, WIP cap, and post-merge acceptance". Full required content:

- An intro binding the section to its design record: these definitions are ratified semantics; every transition contract in this specification is subordinate to them; design record: repo `thewoolleyman/livespec`, `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/research/03-decision-log.md` (decisions 26 and 32), maintainer's verbatim reasoning in `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/conversation/transcript.md`.
- One plain-English sentence per state: `backlog` = captured but not yet groomed — needs decomposition or grooming before it can be worked. `pending-approval` = prepared (groomed) but not yet authorized to start — the lane that shows the maintainer exactly what waits on their GO. `ready` = authorized to start (a human approved it, or the item's `admission_policy: auto` delegation did), pulled into work when a WIP slot frees; an item awaiting a human's permission MUST NOT be in `ready`. `active` = admitted into a WIP slot and being worked. `acceptance` = merged, live, and observable, awaiting post-ship confirmation per its effective `acceptance_policy`. `blocked` = cannot proceed without something outside the factory (`blocked_reason: needs-human` or `infra-external`). `done` = accepted and closed.
- The transition rule: `approve` IS the `pending-approval → ready` transition ("manual: a human; auto: automatic"); approval ≡ `ready` membership; the admission valve (`ready → active`) is purely mechanical; permission was settled upstream at `approve`.
- The maintainer's rationale, verbatim (source: repo `thewoolleyman/livespec`, `plan/archive/work-item-state-machine/conversation/transcript.md`): "If we don't respect the groomed attribute and add autonomous execution, then the factory can just go wild and go completely off track and never stop, piling up a bunch of incorrect work that should have never been performed at the review gates, or even worse if the review gate is automated, pushing it all to production."
- The delegation principle from the same design session: the human always holds the ultimate decision but MAY delegate the GO per item or blanket for low-risk work — that delegation IS `admission_policy` (`auto` = the GO is delegated; `manual` = the human gives it explicitly).
